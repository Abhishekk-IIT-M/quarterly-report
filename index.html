Valuation Model: Black-Scholes

We use the Black-Scholes formula to price options:

$$ C(S, t) = N(d_1)S - N(d_2)Ke^{-r(T-t)} $$

This helps us assess the value of derivatives in our portfolio.

Remember to mention that 'C' is the call option price, 'S' is the current stock price, and 'K' is the strike price. This model is foundational for our risk management strategy.
